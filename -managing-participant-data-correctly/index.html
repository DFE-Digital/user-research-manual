Managing particpant data - introduction

Beta This guidance is in beta. It is likely to change over time.

Your feedback will help improve it

! Warning Not managing personal data correctly is a serious offence

You must always manage the particpant data you generate during your user research under UK GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation).

We have created user research guidance for managing data, with support from the Department’s Office of the Data Protection Officer. This guidance complements DfE’s GDPR guidance and policy that can be found on the intranet.

The UK GDPR guidance on these pages is specific for user researchers and explains how you can apply it to your day to day work.

GDPR is very important in DfE. In fact, we were audited by the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) in 2020. You can read the ICO report yourself and see a number of urgent and high risk priorities that DfE are tackling. If GDPR is not followed, we risk a fine from the ICO and risk reputational damage that could impact research.

Our guidance is not intended to make your work harder, but to protect users, yourself and the Department.
